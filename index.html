About me

I deeply admire nature as it embodies an incredible amount of engineering, even in its tiniest and seemingly simplest creations. Even at the microscopic scale, we can find super-efficient architectures with dedicated functions and even built-in mechanisms for error correction. These natural marvels of engineering inspire me to approach man-made devices from a fundamental level.

With cumulative efforts of science and technology through centuries, we have been able to realize powerful computing machines. Yet, there lies immense potential for hardware-stack optimization. By improving hardware alone, we can often achieve greater efficiency without changing the software-stack. As Richard Feynman famously said, "There's Plenty of Room at the Bottom."

I am passionate about computer architecture, embedded systems, robotics, and operating systems.

I see myself as a lifelong, curious learner—because there's always something to learn, explore and improve upon—and, more often than not, a problem-solver. Oftentimes, in order to find a solution, we just need to trace the point of fault or the underperforming module over which we can iterate enough trials of possible-solution producing better results or uncovering a better approach.

Moreover, I strongly believe that after being gifted with the human life form—supposedly the smartest life form—comes with a responsibility: to dive deep into a domain and, with efforts oriented in the right direction, produce results that will benefit all life forms on our mother Earth and maybe beyond.

When I am not teaching, building, or debugging systems, you'll likely find me hiking, cycling, or occasionally writing poetry—read my poems here.
